About

This is probably a cliché bio, but I'm going for it anyways :P

MY STORY

Art has always been an important aspect of my life since I was a child. It was my favourite subject throughout school, and it is still my favourite thing to do now. Drawing has gotten me through those boring classes and made my notebooks look 10x better than they would have if they were just plain notes. Don't worry, art never hindered my grades, and if anything it brought them up! Jokes aside, I graduated and that's what matters, right? .... NOOO.

I went through a quarter life crisis and I was in a really dark place. I was in a program I didn't like, but at the same time I didn't know what else to go into. (If you're wondering "why not art?", I'll be getting to that soon!) I took a half semester off in hopes to pull my life together, but to no avail.

I felt trapped, lost, confused, and absolutely worthless. I hit rock bottom and felt like giving up so many times. Even though I had my brother and a few friends who I could talk too, I still just felt like they wouldn't care, and that I was a bother to them. So I just kept these feelings bottled up inside me– for a good 1-2 years.

Long story short, it was art that helped me cope. Art was the big factor that helped me overcome that dark moment in my life and helped me express how I was feeling in ways that words just couldn't

WHY I WANT AN APPRENTICESHIP

Originally, art was what I wanted to study, but as an asian child that would have been a disgrace. Let alone tattoos– they were viewed as "taboo" in my household. However, now that I've matured, I say f*ck it! I'm tired of living in fear of what my parents and others think about an art career. It's my life, and I want to live it doing what I love. Reflecting on it now, I realize that the ONE thing that I've always loved, and the ONE thing that I was always fairly decent at, was Art.

Art has always made sense to me, and even though I didn't realize it during my low point, art was there to help me overcome it and grow as an individual. (I still have the pieces that helped me stay strong. However, I don't believe they're the greatest! Always down to show 'em to those who are interested!) The time for change is the present, and if I can make a living with drawing, then I'm going for it! For these reasons, I am looking for a tattoo apprenticeship.

MY ART STYLE

So if it wasn't already obvious, I have always loved art. Especially tattoos because it uses the skin as the canvas. Additionally, I love how the art "attaches" to the person, for a REALLY long time. My favourite tattoo styles are Black and Grey Realism, Illustrative, Asian, and Fineline/Micro tattoos.

My favourite styles to draw are realism, and illustrative. Although I do love challenging myself by drawing things outside my comfort zone! Recently I have been into creating linework designs that combine aspects of realism, illustration, and geometric. My particular style uses black & grey, with occasional hints of red. Additionally, smooth shading and clean edges are what help bring my art to life. Although my drawings tend to be on the small side, I always strive to ensure they have depth and detail.
